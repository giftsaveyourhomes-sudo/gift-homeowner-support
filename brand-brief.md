# Brand Brief

## Business
Free homeowner advocacy and education for Oʻahu homeowners facing foreclosure, probate/inherited homes, tax liens, HOA liens, vacant/distressed property, or general mortgage hardship. Run by Sakawrat "Gift" Kitkuakul, Ph.D. Not a law firm, not a real estate brokerage — a volunteer advocate who explains options in plain language and connects homeowners to licensed professionals when needed. 100% free, no fees, no referral compensation. In some cases Gift may be interested in purchasing a home directly if the owner decides to sell — always disclosed upfront, never pressured.

## Customer
An Oʻahu homeowner who is scared, overwhelmed, or avoiding their mail because they're behind on their mortgage, got a foreclosure notice, inherited a distressed property, or are dealing with a lien. They don't know their options and often assume it's too late to do anything. They're not looking for a sales pitch — they're looking for someone calm to explain what's actually happening and what they can still do about it.

## Voice
Warm and personal, expert and authoritative (Ph.D., decade of financial experience), plain-language and non-salesy. Calm, reassuring, never fear-mongering — the opposite of a hard sell.

## Universal Voice Rules (apply to every post)
- Contractions always ("don't" not "do not")
- Active voice, short sentences
- Address the reader as "you"
- Numbers as digits ("3 tips" not "three tips")
- No em dashes — use commas, periods, or "..."
- One concrete idea per post, not three
- Specific details over generic statements
- Always free, no pressure, no fees — never undercut this with pushy language
