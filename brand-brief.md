# Brand Brief

> Captured: 2026-07-17
> Full authoritative source: `brand-voice-reference.md` (synced from Gift's Dropbox — always defer to that file over this summary if they conflict)

## Business
Free homeowner advocacy and education for Oʻahu homeowners facing foreclosure, probate/inherited homes, tax liens, HOA liens, or vacant/distressed property. Run by Sakawrat "Gift" Kitkuakul, Ph.D. — a volunteer educator and advocate, not an attorney, real estate broker, or HUD-approved counselor. 100% free, no contracts, no fees, no equity taken. Gift may, in some cases, be interested in purchasing a home directly — always disclosed upfront, never pressured.

## Customer
An Oʻahu homeowner who is scared or overwhelmed and often avoiding their own mail because they're behind on their mortgage, got a foreclosure notice, inherited a distressed property, or are dealing with a lien. They usually assume it's too late to do anything. They need a knowledgeable neighbor to explain what's actually happening, not a sales pitch.

## Primary CTA
Text HELP to (808) 215-5828 — but always frame it as "map vs. directions": the content gives the general picture, the call is where they figure out what applies to *their specific situation*. Never end with a generic "call me for help" that doesn't explain what the conversation adds beyond what was just read.

## Strong Opinion / Wedge
Most homeowners in crisis don't lose their homes because there were no options — they lose them because no one told them what the options were. Gift's whole role is fixing that information gap, one conversation at a time. This is the real, sourced wedge — not to be replaced with a manufactured "hot take."

## Story Vault
- "Gift helped me understand my options when I was too scared to open my mail." (use with attribution/permission)
- Facebook cover image tagline already in use: "Don't let the bank take your home. Explore all options."
- "500+ Oʻahu homeowners educated on their options" — verify count before use; soften to "hundreds" if uncertain.

## Voice
The informed neighbor, not a service provider. Educational first, warm and neighbor-first, empathetic before informational, locally rooted (Oʻahu with ʻokina, "talk stories," Aloha), pressure-free, hopeful and empowering. Lead with "here's what you should know," never "here's how I can help you."

**Platform tone differs — this matters:**
- **Facebook**: longer, narrative, story format. Open with a situation a homeowner recognizes, share what they should know, close with an open invitation to talk. NOT a punchy viral hook style.
- **Instagram**: short, punchy, one fact per post, story-driven, 8-12 hashtags.

## Universal Voice Rules (apply to every post)
- Contractions always ("don't" not "do not")
- Active voice, short sentences
- Address the reader as "you"
- Numbers as digits ("3 tips" not "three tips")
- No em dashes — use commas, periods, or "..."
- One concrete idea per post, not three
- Say "neighbors/homeowners/families," never "clients/customers"
- "Oʻahu" always with the ʻokina
- Never: urgency tactics, outcome guarantees, "I'll negotiate with your lender," implying legal/tax advice
