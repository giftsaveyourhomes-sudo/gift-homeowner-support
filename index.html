Know your options

What homeowners in distress should know

Keeping your home: what's possible

Many homeowners don't know that options like loan modification, repayment plans, or hardship programs may still be available — even after receiving a foreclosure notice. Understanding these options is the first step. I help you organize documents and understand the general landscape. For negotiation with lenders or legal proceedings, I connect you with trusted, licensed professionals like attorneys and HUD-approved counselors.

Learning what's available costs nothing — and could change everything.

Selling your home: when and how it works

If selling turns out to be the best path, it helps to understand the timeline, what a fair process looks like, and what questions to ask. I can help you understand your options and connect you with ethical, licensed professionals — including reputable buyers if that's the direction you choose. I can also help with clean-out, preparation, and finding your next place.

No pressure. Ever. You decide. I don't receive referral fees or commissions from anyone I connect you with.

Probate, liens & other situations

Inherited homes, tax liens, and HOA liens each come with their own rules, timelines, and options that most homeowners aren't aware of. Understanding the basics can prevent costly mistakes.

Every situation is different — the goal is to understand yours clearly before making any decisions.

FAQ

Questions homeowners ask most

Is your help really free?

Yes. My homeowner support and resource connections are 100% free for homeowners. There's no obligation and no pressure to sell.

Do I have to sell my home?

No. Understanding your options comes first — keeping the home, pausing foreclosure, negotiating, or selling only if you choose. No one should make that decision without knowing all the alternatives.

What should I bring (or tell you) on the first call?

If you have them: any foreclosure notice, loan servicer/lender name, court case info, auction date, HOA/tax letters, and your best contact details. If you don't have documents, that's okay — we'll start with what you know.

Are you a lawyer or HUD counselor?

No. I'm a homeowner advocate. I share general information to help you understand your situation and options. I don't provide legal or tax advice. When those services are needed, I connect you with trusted, verified professionals.

If there's an urgent deadline, what should I do?

Text or call as soon as possible so we can confirm dates and identify the fastest next steps. If you're in immediate danger of losing housing, contact a HUD-approved counselor or Legal Aid right away.
